Solving Negative Powers and Zero Power

Negative Powers: A negative power means you take the reciprocal (flip the number) and then apply the positive exponent.
Example: 2-3 means "take the reciprocal of 23" Zero Power: Any number raised to the power of zero is always 1, except for 0 itself.
Example: 40 = 1, 100 = 1

This is just a math rule you can remember: any non-zero number to the power of zero equals 1!

Negative Exponent Rules

Summary:

  • The base is the number you multiply.
  • The power tells you how many times to multiply the base.
  • Simplify multiplication by writing it in exponential form.
  • Negative powers mean taking the reciprocal.
  • Anything to the power of zero equals 1.
